Impact of Bike Lanes on Traffic & Emissions

Bike lanes are designed to promote cycling, but they can also reduce road capacity, leading to increased congestion and carbon emissions.

Effects on Traffic Flow

Studies have shown that removing car lanes to make room for bike lanes can lead to longer commute times for drivers.

Example: In Adelaide, new bike lanes on Frome Road have reportedly worsened congestion.

Impact on Carbon Emissions

Longer traffic queues caused by reduced lane space can result in more idling cars, increasing emissions.
